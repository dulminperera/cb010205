Pinnawala

Pinnawala zoo is also mainly an orphanage for elephants who are no longer able to perform their purpose or feeding newborn baby elephants. This is in Rambukkana. Tourists and locals can visit this under a payment and indeed this trip is a worthy experience. You as tourists will be able to see baby elephants being fed, taken care of, and being washed which is very photogenic as a tourist. This Is also a world widely recognized orphanage since the 1982 and it said there is about 93 elephants and 20 of them are being bred here and counting.

yala

Yala National Park is the best way to experience wildlife in Sri Lanka. As in from the very second you enter the Yala nation park u will see birds in the lunch green forests with unrouted roads going from start to end covering every part on the forest. This does not include very rare animals but any animal in the island can be seen here and up close. There will be many safari guides, guiding you from start to end and showing the best places. Yala is filled with so many lakes and water bodies so the animals can feed on. Trees and forests play its role by keeping the animals sheltered.

Sinharaja

Sinharaja is a lowland and comparatively a very lush and more dark green forest to others. This rainforest holds up to 50% of Sri Lanka species. This includes birds, mammals, Scorpio, reptiles, insects, and lizards being the main species. The trees in here keeps the animals safe from any danger in the animal cycle, it is stated that 60% of the trees are rare and someone of them are only limited to this rain forest making this a very rare rain forest and holds much value to Sri Lanka. Now this rainforest which is also the home to many rare trees and animal species has a maximum distance e of 81k from north to south with an area size of 88.64km
