Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka, the tropical island located under the southern part of India. As the rest of the world says, “Pearl of the Indian Ocean”. The beauty of Sri Lanka doesn’t stop there, it indeed is an Island filled with mountainous forests filled with deep green lush and sandy beaches coving every bit of the coast. Sri Lanka isn’t just filled with greenery and breathtaking views, but also with a rich culture ageing back 100s of years.

Religion

Sri Lanka is an Island where the whole population follows 4 major religions
  1. Buddhism
  2. Muslim
  3. Christianity
  4. Tamil

Buddhism being the major religion followed by many Sri Lankan which is around 70.2% out of the total population and the rest of the percentage being divided over the 3 majorly followed religions. The nice thing about this is each Sri Lankan citizen celebrates one another religion when it comes to celebrating their holy days/ festivals. This also includes every citizen having an open mind about ones another religion, culture and lifestyle meaning these spreads more of a peaceful society to each Sri Lankan living and all visitors are welcomed with opened hands to embrace their families’ customs.

Spices

Starting from the history, the Sri Lankan cuisine was very much based off natural spices when it comes to flavoring the dishes . Indeed, a Sri Lankan meal can light up your taste buds like no other which is why it is a must try! When visited. The space palette is so vastly spread that around 30,000 tons of spices s exported to international borders. This includes.
  • Ceylon Cinnamon
  • Ceylon Pepper
  • Ceylon Cloves
  • Ceylon Cardamon
  • Ceylon Nutmeg
Each spice got its own flavor, smell and shape meaning it has its own role when it comes to making the dish. The geographical location of Sri Lanka plays a major role of having to grow this many spices with its consistent rain for certain areas and harsh sunlight at places.

Languages

Sinhala and Tamil are the most widely spoken languages across the island making it the main two languages of Sri Lanka. Sinhala is mainly spoken by Sinhalese which adds up to around 16 million citizens from the total population making it their mother tongue and is also used by other religions. Looking back at the roots and the history of the Sinhala language, it was with the Indo-European group of scripts of the Indo-Aryan branch. Sinhala language got its own way of writing which adds up cultural value to the country to have its own language. Second being the Tamil language, it is mainly used by the people in the Northern part of Sri Lanka whereas the major population of the northern are Tamils coming also from the northern part of India and Tamil being their mother tongue. This community of Sri Lanka adds up to about 28.5% of Sri Lanka which is 4.7 million citizens in numbers. English is also being used by many citizens of Sri Lanka and widely spoken around in the younger generations.
