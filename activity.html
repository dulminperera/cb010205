Coral Reef Hikkaduwa

Hikkaduwa National Park is one of Sri Lanka's three maritime national parks. A bordering coral reef with a high degree of biodiversity can be found in the national park. On May 18, 1979, the region was designated as a wildlife sanctuary, and on August 14, 1988, it was upgraded to a nature reserve with expanded land area. In the next 25 years, the number of visitors will increase, causing the coral reef to deteriorate. On September 19, 2002, the reef was designated as a national park in order to mitigate the effects on the ecology.

Whale Watching

From November to April, whales congregate in the waters off the coast of south-western Sri Lanka. Secure your spot on a whale-watching cruise from Mirissa with this Hikkaduwa excursion, and save valuable holiday time. After an air-conditioned ride to the pier, head out to sea to spot blue, fin, and sperm whales, as well as dolphins, while your boat maintains a safe distance and a naturalist provides information. Transfers to or from your Hikkaduwa hotel are included.
